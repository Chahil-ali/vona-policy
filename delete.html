Delete your Vona data

Vona — voice-first AI journal by A&S Dev Studio

Your journal is stored on your phone

Vona has no user accounts and keeps no copy of your journal on any server. All entries, photos, voice recordings, places and settings exist only on your device.

How to delete your data

  1. Delete entries in the app: open an entry and tap the trash icon. Deleted entries move to "Recently Deleted" and are erased forever after 30 days — or immediately if you choose Delete forever, which also removes the entry's photo and audio files from your phone.
  2. Delete everything: uninstalling the Vona app permanently removes all app data (journal, media, settings, PIN) from your device.
  3. Server-side records: to request deletion of the small amount of data that can exist outside your phone (see below), email us at alichahil047@gmail.com with the subject "Vona data deletion request". We process requests within 30 days.

What is deleted and what is kept

  • Journal content (text, audio, photos, places): stored only on your device — deleted by you in the app or by uninstalling. Content sent to the AI service is processed only to generate a response and is not stored by us.
  • Anonymous device ID and usage counters: deleted on request via email.
  • Subscription purchase records: processed by RevenueCat and Google Play. Deleted from RevenueCat on request via email; Google Play keeps its own billing records according to Google's policies (we cannot delete those for you).

No other data about you is held. There is no additional retention period beyond the ones described above.
